Course description

Learning objective

  • Personally, I hope this course will help you have fun and nerdy conversations with friends (or strangers on the bus), whether they’re math people or not. At the least, if you don’t like the awkward silence, strike a conversation about $\infty$!
  • Professionally, I hope you will find beauty in mathematics by knowing that mathematics is all about ideas, not computations (although computations play a big part in the usefulness of mathematics).

Class Policies

Lectures

  • If you sleep, please don’t snore. (Thanks Gautam Iyer for this amazing policy!)
  • Please be respectful to your classmates.

Homework

  • Homework must be turned in at the beginning of class on the due date.
  • Generously credit all of the people who you collaborate with at the beginning of your work.
  • If you use outside sources (internet, books, friends, etc.) for a particular problem, acknowledge them at the beginning of the problem. You will NOT be penalized for consulting outside sources as long as you credit them.
  • Late homework policy:
    • Late homework will not be accepted.
    • Worst homework will be dropped.
    • Exceptions will be considered if you have documented personal emergencies lasting at least 18 days.
  • Advice:
    • Eat well and get enough sleep.
    • Start early. One problem per day is more pleasant than seven problems in one night.
    • Collaboration is strongly encouraged but you MUST write up your own work. Word-to-word copying is plagiarism.
    • Try to understand the materials rather than rote memorization. This will show in exams.
    • Try to write clearly and demonstrate clarity of thoughts.
